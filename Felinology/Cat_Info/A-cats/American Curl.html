American Curl

Description

The American Curl is characterized by its unusual aural arrangement. Their famously curly ears have got them their name. The ears of the Curl cats are twisted at the back, pointing head-wards. However, these lap cats are never born with these curls. The curl starts to show up after a week, and continues until the kitten attains the age of four, after which, the process stops.

Overview

  • Origin: United States of America
  • Preffered Food: The daily diet routine of the American Curl should be very simple and cat-like, full of balanced nutrients like all other cats need. But American Curls need to eat a lot of fish and meat for protein and fat. Your cat does not need lots of carbohydrate like the dogs do, since cats are animals that are not as active as the dogs. Insist on raw meat.

Fun Facts:

  • The American Curls are often called the "Peter Pan" of the cat world.
  • The ears of the American Curl are expressive. They would perk up in curiosity, swivel for listening, and twitch back in annoyance.
